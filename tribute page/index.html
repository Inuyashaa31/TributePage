Timeline

1971

Elon Musk is born in South Africa to an engineer father and a model mother.

1983

Writes first video game called Blastar; sells for $500.

1995-1991

Attends college at Queen’s University in Kingston, Ontario. Then transfers to the University of Pennsylvania; completed a BS in Economics (Wharton) and a BA with a major in physics

1995

Moves to Silicon Valley; defers graduate program in applied physics and materials science at Stanford University to join the Internet boom

1999

Sells Zip2 to Compaq, the personal computer company, for $307 million, of which $22 million went to Musk. Then forms X.com, which in 2000 morphs into PayPal

July 2002

eBay acquires PayPal for $1.5 billion in stock, of which $165 million goes to Musk

May 2002

Founds SpaceX to design, manufacture and launche advanced rockets and spacecraft.

2004

Invests several millions into Tesla Motors

2007

SpaceX wins $1.6 billion contract to bring cargo to the International Space Station

October 2008

Becomes Tesla’s CEO

June 29, 2010

Tesla IPO

May 2012

SpaceX becomes the first commercial vehicle to deliver a load of supplies to the International Space Station.

June 2012

Tesla begins deliveries of the all-electric Model S.

August 2013

Releases sketch and concept of the Hyperloop

2017

Tesla releases the Model 3. A cheaper version of the previous Model S and X.

May 30, 2020

SpaceX successfully launches 2 astronauts into space on the Crew Dragon in order to dock with the ISS.

Elon @ SpaceX

Elon Musk

Elon Musk is a South African-born American entrepreneur and businessman who founded X.com in 1999 (which later became PayPal), SpaceX in 2002 and Tesla Motors in 2003. Musk became a multimillionaire in his late 20s when he sold his start-up company, Zip2, to a division of Compaq Computers. He has an estimated net-worth of around 59.2 billion dollars and is listed as the 16th richest person in the world according to Forbes. His ultimate goal is to make life multiplanentary and help humanity explore beyond Earth.
